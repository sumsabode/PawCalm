## Brand Overview

PawCalm is an AI-powered behavioral pattern interpreter that helps dog owners understand their pet's behavioral changes and make informed decisions about when veterinary intervention is needed. We exist to be the calm in every pet parent's worried moment.

### Brand Promise
"We don't diagnose your dog — we calm your worry and help you decide what to do next."

### Brand Personality
- **Calming** — We reduce anxiety, never amplify it. Every interaction should feel like a deep breath.
- **Empathetic** — We understand the guilt, the spiraling worry, the "am I overreacting?" moments.
- **Trustworthy** — We never overstep into diagnosis. We're clear about what we are and what we aren't.
- **Warm** — We speak like a knowledgeable friend, not a clinical database.
- **Personal** — We always use the dog's name. We remember history. We treat every dog as unique.

### Color Usage Rules
- The recommendation colors (Green, Amber, Red) are **reserved exclusively** for the tri-level guidance system. Do not use Monitor Green for generic success states outside of assessment results.
- PawCalm Teal is the dominant brand color — use it for primary CTAs, the app bar, and brand accents.
- Soft Cream (#FFF7ED) is the default app background, not pure white. This creates warmth and reduces clinical feel.
- Never use harsh contrasts or flashing/pulsing color animations. The UI should feel soft and settled.

### Typography

**Primary Typeface:** Nunito
- Use for all UI elements, body text, and general communications
- Weights: Regular (400), Medium (500), SemiBold (600), Bold (700)
- Rounded letterforms reinforce warmth and approachability

**Secondary Typeface:** Nunito Sans
- Use for smaller UI text, metadata, disclaimers, and data displays
- Weights: Regular (400), Medium (500), SemiBold (600)

**Why Nunito:** Rounded sans-serif fonts feel friendlier and less clinical than geometric or grotesque alternatives. This is deliberate — PawCalm should never feel like a medical tool.

### Type Scale

## Logo Usage

### Primary Logo
The PawCalm logo combines a stylized paw print with a gentle wave/calm motion underneath, representing both pets and emotional relief. The logo should appear in PawCalm Teal (#0D9488) on light backgrounds or white on dark backgrounds.

### App Icon
- Rounded square with PawCalm Teal background
- White paw print mark centered
- No text in app icon — mark only

### Clear Space
Maintain clear space around the logo equal to the height of the paw print on all sides.

### Minimum Size
- Digital: 28px height minimum
- Print: 0.5 inch height minimum

### Don'ts
- Don't rotate or distort the logo
- Don't change the logo colors outside approved variations
- Don't place the logo on busy or dark photographic backgrounds
- Don't add effects (shadows, gradients, outlines)
- Don't use red or amber versions of the logo (these colors are reserved for recommendations)

## Voice & Tone

### Voice Characteristics
1. **Calm** — Measured, reassuring, never panicked. Even "Call Vet" messages remain composed.
2. **Knowledgeable** — Speak with authority about dog behavior, but always frame as guidance, not diagnosis.
3. **Personal** — Always use the dog's name. Reference their profile and history when applicable.
4. **Honest** — Be transparent about uncertainty. "We don't know for sure" is better than false confidence.
5. **Warm** — Like a trusted friend who happens to know a lot about dogs.

### Tone Variations by Context

**Assessment Results**
- Lead with acknowledgment of the owner's worry
- Use the dog's name in every response
- Present information as possibilities, never certainties
- End with a clear next step
- Example: "It's completely understandable to worry when Luna skips a meal. Here's what might be going on..."

**Monitor (Green) Recommendations**
- Reassuring and grounding
- Normalize the behavior when appropriate
- Give specific things to watch for (makes owner feel proactive, not passive)
- Example: "This is very common and usually resolves on its own. Keep an eye on whether Luna returns to her normal routine by tomorrow."

**Try This (Amber) Recommendations**
- Supportive and action-oriented
- Provide specific, doable interventions
- Frame as "things that often help" not "you must do this"
- Example: "Here are a few things that often help in situations like this. Try them over the next day or two and see how Bella responds."

**Call Vet (Red) Recommendations**
- Calm but clear. Urgency without panic.
- Never say "emergency" unless truly life-threatening
- Provide questions to bring to the vet
- Example: "Based on what you've described, it's a good idea to check in with your vet soon. Here are some questions to bring with you."

**Error Messages**
- Acknowledge the problem gently
- Reassure that their data is safe
- Provide a clear path forward
- Example: "Something went wrong on our end — your concern details are saved. Try again in a moment, or reach out to us if it keeps happening."

**Onboarding**
- Warm and welcoming
- Celebrate the new profile creation ("Welcome, Luna!")
- Set expectations clearly about what PawCalm is and isn't
- Example: "Let's get to know your pup! The more we know about Luna's normal, the better we can help when something seems off."

**Loading / Processing States**
- Calming, never anxiety-inducing
- Use reassuring microcopy, not technical language
- Good: "Analyzing patterns..." / "Reviewing Luna's profile..."
- Bad: "Processing request..." / "Loading..." / spinning wheels

**Disclaimers**
- Always present, never hidden
- Brief and clear, not legalistic
- Standard: "This is general guidance and not a substitute for veterinary advice."

### Words We Use
- "Guidance" (not "diagnosis")
- "What might be happening" (not "what's wrong")
- "Check in with your vet" (not "go to the ER")
- "Concern" (not "symptom" or "problem")
- "Your dog's name" (always personalized)
- "Monitor," "Try This," "Call Vet" (the official recommendation labels)

### Words We Avoid
- "Diagnose," "diagnosis," "condition," "disease" (we don't diagnose)
- "Emergency" (unless truly life-threatening — difficulty breathing, seizures, collapse)
- "Nothing to worry about" (we never dismiss concerns)
- "You should" (we suggest and guide, not prescribe)
- "Obviously" or "clearly" (nothing is obvious when you're worried)
- Clinical jargon without explanation

## Photography & Imagery

### Photo Style
- Natural lighting, warm tones — home environments, parks, everyday moments
- Real dogs in candid, relaxed poses (not studio shots)
- Diverse representation of dog owners and breeds
- Moments of connection between owner and dog
- Avoid: clinical/veterinary settings, distressed animals, sterile environments

### Illustration Style
- Soft, rounded shapes with organic lines
- Warm color palette drawn from brand colors
- Friendly and calming, not cartoonish or overly cute
- Use to explain the assessment flow, onboarding steps, and recommendation levels
- Calming animations for loading states (gentle breathing motion, soft pulses — never spinning)

### Icons
- Use Lucide React icons (rounded style) for consistency
- 24px default size
- 1.5px stroke weight
- Match icon color to surrounding text or use PawCalm Teal for emphasis
- Recommendation icons: Use filled circles (🟢 🟡 🔴) for the tri-level system

## Writing Guidelines

### Headlines
- Lead with emotional benefit, not feature
- Use sentence case
- Keep under 8 words when possible
- Good: "Peace of mind in every worried moment"
- Bad: "AI-Powered Behavioral Pattern Analysis Engine"

### Body Copy
- Use second person ("you") to speak directly to the pet parent
- Always include the dog's name when contextually available
- Keep paragraphs short (2-3 sentences max)
- Avoid passive voice
- Reading level: aim for grade 6-8 (accessible under stress)

### CTAs
- Start with action verb
- Be specific and calming
- Good: "Log a concern" / "See what's happening" / "Save to history"
- Bad: "Submit" / "Click here" / "Analyze"
- The primary CTA "Log a Concern" must always be prominent and accessible

### Sensitivity Guidelines
- Never trivialize a pet parent's worry ("it's probably nothing" is forbidden)
- Never guilt-trip about vet visits ("you should have gone sooner")
- Never claim to replace veterinary care
- Acknowledge that the human-animal bond is profound and real
- Be aware of the emotional stakes — people deeply love their dogs

## Component Patterns

### Buttons
- Primary: PawCalm Teal background, white text, rounded corners
- Secondary: White background, PawCalm Teal border and text
- Destructive: Call Vet Red background, white text (rare — only for account deletion)
- Border radius: 12px (rounded, approachable)
- Padding: 12px 20px
- Min-height: 48px (touch-friendly)
- Min-width: 120px

### Cards
- Cloud White background
- 1px border in Warm Gray
- Border radius: 16px (generous rounding)
- Shadow: 0 2px 8px rgba(0,0,0,0.06) (soft, subtle)
- Padding: 20px

### Assessment Result Cards
- Use the appropriate soft background color (Soft Green/Amber/Red BG)
- Recommendation badge at top with corresponding color
- Border-left: 4px solid in recommendation color
- Border radius: 16px
- Padding: 24px

### Concern Wizard (5-Step Flow)
- One question per screen (reduces cognitive load)
- Progress bar at top in PawCalm Teal
- Large tap targets (minimum 48px height)
- Back button always visible
- "Skip" option in Medium Gray for optional fields
- Transitions: smooth fade (200ms), no abrupt changes

### Forms
- Labels above inputs, in Medium weight
- Required fields marked with subtle asterisk
- Help text below inputs in Small size, Medium Gray
- Error states: Call Vet Red border, error message below
- Focus state: 2px PawCalm Teal outline
- Input border radius: 12px
- Input padding: 14px 16px
- Input min-height: 48px

### Navigation
- Bottom tab bar (iOS/Android pattern)
- Tabs: Home, History, Profile, Settings
- Active state: PawCalm Teal icon + label
- Inactive state: Medium Gray icon + label
- "Log a Concern" FAB (Floating Action Button) optionally overlaid on bottom nav

### Recommendation Badges
```
🟢 Monitor    — Background: #22C55E, Text: white, Border radius: 24px (pill)
🟡 Try This   — Background: #F59E0B, Text: white, Border radius: 24px (pill)
```

## Motion & Animation

### Principles
- **Calming over flashy** — All motion should reduce anxiety, not add visual noise
- **Purposeful** — Animation only when it communicates state change or guides attention
- **Smooth** — Ease-in-out curves, never sharp or bouncy

### Specific Guidelines
- Page transitions: Fade or gentle slide (200-300ms)
- Loading/processing: Slow breathing pulse animation in PawCalm Teal (opacity 0.4 → 1.0, 2s cycle)
- Result reveal: Gentle fade-in from top, staggered sections (100ms delay between)
- Never use: Spinners, shaking/vibrating elements, rapid blinking, confetti
- Intentional delay on processing screen: 2-3 seconds minimum (builds trust that analysis is happening)

## Accessibility

### Color Contrast
- All text must meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards
- Body text: minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio
- Large text (18px+): minimum 3:1 contrast ratio
- Recommendation colors always paired with text labels (never color alone)

### Interactive Elements
- All interactive elements must be keyboard accessible
- Focus states must be clearly visible (2px PawCalm Teal outline)
- Touch targets: minimum 48px × 48px (exceeds standard 44px for stressed users)
- Provide text alternatives for all icons

### Screen Readers
- All images need descriptive alt text
- Use semantic HTML elements
- Maintain logical heading hierarchy
- Recommendation badges must announce both the label and meaning (e.g., "Monitor — low urgency")
- Announce dynamic content changes, especially assessment results

### Stress-Aware Design
- Larger-than-standard touch targets (48px minimum)
- Generous whitespace between interactive elements
- No time-limited interactions (users may be distracted by their dog)
- High-contrast mode support for outdoor use

## Usage Examples

### Correct Usage
✓ "PawCalm helps you understand what your dog's behavior might mean."
✓ "Log a concern" (specific, calming CTA)
✓ Using PawCalm Teal for primary actions
✓ Warm, natural photos of dogs and their owners
✓ "It's understandable to worry when Max isn't eating." (empathetic, uses dog's name)

### Incorrect Usage
✗ "PawCalm diagnoses your dog's health issues using AI." (we don't diagnose)
✗ "Submit" (vague, clinical CTA)
✗ Using recommendation colors for non-recommendation purposes
✗ Stock photos of dogs in clinical/vet settings
✗ "It's probably nothing, don't worry." (dismissive of concern)
✗ "Your dog has anxiety disorder." (diagnosing)

1. **Home** — Dog profile summary, prominent "Log a Concern" CTA, recent assessments
2. **Concern Wizard** — 5-step guided flow (one question per screen, progress bar)
3. **Processing** — Calming animation with reassuring microcopy (2-3 sec delay)
4. **Results** — Recommendation badge, expandable explanations, action items, disclaimer
5. **History** — Timeline of past assessments with filters
6. **Dog Profile** — Photo, name, breed, age, weight, health info, normal patterns

1. Recommendation badge component (Green/Amber/Red pill)
2. Concern wizard step component (question + input + progress)
3. Assessment result card
4. Dog profile card (compact for home, full for profile screen)
